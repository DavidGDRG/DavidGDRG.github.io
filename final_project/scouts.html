Scouts

----- Boy Scouts, which has been recently renamed to just Scouts, is an organization that brings youth together and teaches them leadership skills that they use in the future. I have been a part of Scouts since I was in 3'rd grade. I've learned important skills such as cooking, leading a group of people, wilderness survival and first aid, aquatics safety/rescue and many more. I joined scouts for the fun of camping and the different things I could learn. I'm currently in the tenderfoot rank and plan on getting first class before I age out.

High Adventure #1

----- High adventures are big trips that troops take that usually are several weeks long. The hardest and most physically straining one I have been on so far is called Philmont. Philmont is a almost 2 week long camping trip over the summer where you are flown out to Utah and then to the Philmont mountains in New Mexico. You carry all of your camping gear, tents, food, cooking, supplies and trash on you during the trip. There are toilets at every campsite but not so much along the trails but, usually you have to dig a hole or go in a bush if you need to use the bathroom. I was in a crew of 8 people, we hiked for 9 days and did 84 miles, saw many amazing views and sceneries and I would most definitely do it all over again.

High Adventure #2

----- Another High Adventure that I've been on is called GOBA (Great Ohio Biking Adventure). It was over the summer of 2024 and consisted of riding a bike from city-to-city. My troop did multiple practice training rides leading up to the high adventure to prepare everyone for the pain and strain on their legs. At the start of the GOBA ride my group got into a big crash. 2 people collided in the front resulting in minor injuries, an adult on a sitting bike had to suddenly stop and turned sideways, causing me to crash into them and flipping them and myself over. Then another adult crashed into us causing my bike to go on top of me and sending them flying forward. The sitting bike got disabled for the rest of the GOBA ride and me and the other adult sustained a lot of road rashes and bruises. It was fun but painful and I would only do it again if it was less hot.

Marching Band

----- I have been in marching band for 3 years now and have been a big part of my high school life. It has helped me find new friendships that I would have never had if I wasn't in it. I was able to learn and play a new instrument, a mellophone (marching french horn). I have been to too many football games and so many competitions most in-state but others outside of Ohio, mainly in indiana. Every year we have a trip to Indy where we go perform at Lucas Oil Stadium for our Super Regional Competition. This past Indy trip we placed 33'rd out of 87 different bands. I have sustained many injuries because of marching band, such as a few sprained ankles, hearing problems and neck/back problems. My favorite show that I've been a part of was my very first year during the Everest show. The music was amazing and the visuals were too. I wish I did marching band my freshman year but I'm also glad I didn't. Because if I did I would have even more injuries now. The 3 shows that I've been in are As I mentioned before Everest, my sophomore year. Maps and Legends, my junior year. Then Relic, my senior year.

Athletic Band

----- Athletic Band is where you play during basketball games instead of football games. It's indoors and a lot more relaxing, It only costs $10-$15 to participate in. You get to play a whole lot more fun toons such as Land of 1000 dances, 25 or 6 to 4, BuckJump, and Bad Romance. There are usually a lot less people who participate in it which is actually perfect and anyone can join it no matter if they were in marching band before or not. Game days are usually only on Thursday or Friday nights every week or every other week. It's usually more fun and chill, you can play music and hangout with friends. You don't have a bulky uniform on that is just a boiler either. I love Athletic Band, and it'll always be my favorite "sport" that rivals or just overcomes Marching Band.
